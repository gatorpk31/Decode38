1. Educational Purpose — Not Legal or Medical Advice

Decode38 is an educational tool designed to help veterans and service members understand the language gap between everyday descriptions and VA compensation terminology. Nothing on this site constitutes legal advice, medical advice, a benefits determination, or the establishment of an attorney-client or provider-patient relationship.

Estimated compensation levels are generated from self-reported responses applied against 38 CFR Part 4 criteria. They are not official VA ratings and do not account for the full evidentiary record, C&P examination findings, or adjudicator discretion. Always consult an accredited VSO representative, VA-accredited claims agent, or VA-accredited attorney before filing a claim.

2. Your Legal Rights — Protection from Predatory Claim Services

Federal law protects you from anyone charging upfront fees for VA claims assistance. Under 38 U.S.C. § 5904 and 38 CFR Part 14, accredited claims agents and attorneys may only charge fees after a claim is decided, and only on past-due benefits (back pay) awarded. Any person or company charging you money before your claim is decided is violating federal law.

Your rights as a veteran claimant:

  • You have the right to free claims assistance from any accredited VSO representative (DAV, VFW, American Legion, AMVETS, and others).
  • You have the right to file directly with VA at no cost through va.gov.
  • Accredited representatives may only be paid from retroactive (back pay) benefits awarded — never upfront.
  • Maximum attorney/agent fees are regulated by VA and subject to approval.
  • You can verify whether someone is VA-accredited at va.gov/ogc.
  • To report a predatory claim service, contact the VA Office of General Counsel at 1-800-827-1000 or your state attorney general.

Decode38 will never charge fees tied to your claim outcome, refer you to any service that charges upfront fees, or profit from the outcome of your VA claim.
